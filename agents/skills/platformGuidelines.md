# Platform Guidelines

## TikTok

### Caption
- Maximum 150 characters
- First line IS the hook — write it like the opening line of a great story
- No hashtags in the caption body; they go in the `hashtags` array
- Speak in first person, present tense — "I just learned..." not "In this video..."
- Avoid marketing language: "amazing", "incredible", "must-watch"

### Hashtags
- 3–5 hashtags
- Mix: 1 broad (#productivity), 1 niche (#deepworkmethod), 1 trending if relevant
- Include the `#` prefix in every tag

---

## Instagram Reels

### Caption
- 2–3 sentences maximum
- Open with a statement, not a question
- Close with a genuine question that invites a comment (not "What do you think?")
- Conversational tone — like texting a knowledgeable friend
- Maximum 300 characters

### Hashtags
- 10–15 hashtags
- Include the `#` prefix in every tag
- Mix sizes: 3–4 large (>1M posts), 5–6 medium (100K–1M), 3–4 small (<100K)

---

## YouTube Shorts

### Title
- Maximum 60 characters
- Front-load the primary keyword — it's what appears in search
- No "How to" openers (they test poorly for Shorts)
- Use numbers when the content supports it: "5 Things...", "The #1 Mistake..."
- No clickbait that the video doesn't deliver on

### Description
- 2–3 sentences
- First sentence restates the hook/value proposition with keywords
- Second sentence adds context or a secondary benefit
- Final sentence: CTA to the full video — "Full episode linked in bio" or "Watch the full breakdown at [channel name]"
- Maximum 200 characters

### Tags
- 8–10 tags
- No `#` prefix — these are metadata tags, not hashtags
- Mix broad and specific terms relevant to the content
